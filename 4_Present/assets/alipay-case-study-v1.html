17 Insight

Different UX preferences

International users are accustomed to a significantly more simplified UX in financial products — an obvious contrast to the "all-in-one" squeezed UX designed for domestic China.

What we (Alipay) use

  • Dense, multi-function homepage — everything visible at once.
  • Multiple parallel task paths on a single screen.
  • Minimal white space — information compression as norm.
  • Feature grid with many entry points simultaneously.
Alipay domestic screen

What they (international users) use

  • Single-task screens — one primary action per view.
  • Generous spacing — information is spaced, not compressed.
  • Linear, sequential flows — step by step over all at once.
  • Minimal visual noise — restrained palette, clear hierarchy.
International fintech screens
18 Insight

UX differences stem from social ideology, culture, and language

Interface density is both a linguistic and a social construct — not just a layout preference.

Collectivism (China)

  • People absorb and process massive amounts of information daily.
  • Chinese characters carry more meaning per character — density is natural.
  • Shared context reduces the need for explicit explanation.

Individualism (West)

  • Interfaces are expected to be simple, focused, task-oriented.
  • English requires more characters for the same meaning — needs more space.
  • Less shared context means more explicit guidance needed.

Design implication

More space, less compression, simpler sequencing — the redesign needed to accommodate English's information density requirements.
