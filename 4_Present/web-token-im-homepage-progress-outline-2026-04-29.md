# web.token.im Homepage Revamp Progress Outline

Date: 2026-04-29  
Audience: leadership review  
Goal: fit a 10-15 minute presentation and leave time for demo

## Suggested Flow

### Slide 1: Title

- `imToken Web Homepage Revamp: Progress Review`
- Subtitle: `Current issues, direction exploration, and prototype progress`

### Slide 2: Why This Matters

Key message:

- the homepage has strong product potential, but product value, campaign operation, and discovery flow are not aligned yet.

Talk track:

- Spatial View is differentiated but under-informative;
- List View is more operable but less distinctive;
- onboarding exists but the AA advantage is not clearly framed.

### Slide 3: Current Portal Issues

Suggested title:

- `Current Gaps`

Group the issues into 4 points:

1. `Value proposition is not clear fast enough`
   - users see token visuals before understanding why imToken Web matters.

2. `Campaign surface exists, but campaign logic is weak`
   - List View has posters and featured collections, but weak CTA/context;
   - Spatial View lacks equivalent campaign support.

3. `Spatial and List split capability unevenly`
   - one is stronger for brand, the other is stronger for operation;
   - they do not yet work as one homepage system.

4. `Collection information and onboarding framing are too thin`
   - collections do not provide enough reason to click;
   - lightweight passkey onboarding is not clearly positioned as an advantage.

### Slide 4: Design Direction Framework

Key message:

- we explored 3 parallel directions, not 3 visual variations of the same layout.

Direction summary:

1. `Direction 1: Discovery-first`
   - strongest response to weak collection motivation and campaign understanding.

2. `Direction 2: Merged Spatial/List with collection sidebar`
   - strongest response to the split between Spatial and List.

3. `Direction 3: Activation-first`
   - strongest response to weak onboarding clarity and conversion confidence.

### Slide 5: Direction 1 - Discovery-First

Problem addressed:

- users do not get enough reason to open collections.

Design response:

- richer collection cards;
- clearer campaign modules;
- stronger decision layer before deeper navigation.

Why it matters:

- improves scan-level understanding;
- makes discovery more actionable.

Screenshot placeholder:

- `[Insert Direction 1 key screen]`

### Slide 6: Direction 2 - Merged Spatial/List

Problem addressed:

- Spatial and List currently feel like uneven, separate experiences.

Design response:

- one shared shell for Spatial and List;
- persistent collection sidebar;
- same selected collection remains visible across mode switch;
- campaign hub or featured collection selected by default.

Why it matters:

- preserves Spatial differentiation;
- adds the information layer Spatial currently lacks;
- is the strongest current V2 prototype insight.

Screenshot placeholders:

- `[Insert Direction 2 Spatial mode screenshot]`
- `[Insert Direction 2 List mode screenshot]`

### Slide 7: Direction 3 - Activation-First

Problem addressed:

- the homepage does not clearly communicate lightweight AA onboarding.

Design response:

- stronger first-viewport CTA;
- passkey reassurance;
- 3 capability cues;
- 15s onboarding demo;
- clearer route to first meaningful action.

Why it matters:

- improves conversion confidence;
- turns onboarding into a visible product advantage.

### Slide 8: What We Learned So Far

Suggested title:

- `Early Readout`

Key takeaways:

- richer information clearly improves collection motivation;
- the merged Spatial/List shell looks promising for resolving the current mode split;
- activation-first framing is strongest when the goal is to explain the product advantage quickly.
